Day One

Saturday

8:30 AM Breakfast

Paakashala

Mysore Highway

Clean, reliable stop on the Mysore highway. Easy parking and fresh South Indian food to start the journey.

What to expect

  • Pure veg chain — reliable and hygienic
  • Order the Masala Dosa or Idli-Vada combo
  • Filter coffee is a must before hitting the road
  • Clean restrooms and ample parking
Easy parking & access
11:30 AM Sightseeing

Namdroling Monastery

Bylakuppe — Golden Temple

The largest Tibetan Buddhist settlement in India. Golden statues, intricate murals, and a serene atmosphere that feels worlds away.

What to do here

  • Walk through the three towering golden Buddha statues inside
  • Admire the hand-painted murals covering every wall and ceiling
  • Sit in the courtyard and soak in the peaceful chanting
  • Pick up Tibetan souvenirs and prayer flags from the stalls outside
Entirely flat & paved — very easy to walk
1:00 PM Lunch & Coffee

Fifth Generation Cafe

Kushalnagar, Coorg

A 5th-generation Coorg coffee family's estate-to-cup cafe. Wood-fired pizzas, artisanal brews, and a chill vibe surrounded by coffee plants.

What to do here

  • Try their single-origin pour-over or cold brew
  • Order a wood-fired pizza — surprisingly great for Coorg
  • Walk around the small estate garden after lunch
  • Pick up freshly roasted beans or cocoa products as gifts
3:00 PM Check-in

Madikeri Stay

Your Home Base

Drop off bags and rest after the morning drive. Recharge before the evening unfolds.

What to do here

  • Freshen up and have some tea or coffee on the patio
  • Take a short nap — the evening has a beautiful sunset ahead
  • Enjoy the misty valley views from the property
5:30 PM Sunset

Raja Seat Garden

Short drive from stay

The kings of Kodagu chose this spot for a reason — incredible valley views and the most spectacular sunsets in the Western Ghats.

What to do here

  • Arrive 30 mins before sunset to grab a bench with a view
  • Walk through the flower garden and musical fountain area
  • Watch the clouds roll through the valley below — magical at golden hour
  • Entry is just INR 20 per person
Plenty of benches, no climbing
7:30 PM Dinner

Tiger Tiger

Raja Seat Road, Madikeri

A cozy family-run home turned restaurant. Mom cooks, son Apanna serves. Traditional slow-cooked Coorgi meals in a quiet backyard setting.

What to do here

  • Must try: Pandi Curry (pork) with Kadambuttu (rice dumplings)
  • Chat with Apanna — he'll walk you through the menu and the story behind each dish
  • Closed on Wednesdays — we're going Saturday, so we're good
  • Small portions so order a few dishes to share family-style

"The land of coffee, mist, and stories whispered through the trees."

Day Two

Sunday

9:30 AM Breakfast

Beans N Brews at Raintree

Heritage House, Madikeri

A heritage house with a relaxed patio. Very cozy atmosphere for a slow morning — no rush, just warmth and good food.

What to expect

  • Sit on the open-air patio under the Raintree canopy
  • Order the Coorgi breakfast platter if available
  • Great coffee — fitting for a place in India's coffee country
  • No rush — take your time, the plantation tour starts at 11
11:00 AM Activity

Mercara Gold Estate

Coffee Plantation — 8 km from Madikeri

A 25-year-old coffee and spice farm with Arabica, Robusta, and Liberica varieties. Guided tour, museum, and estate-fresh brews.

What to do here

  • Take the guided estate walk — see coffee, pepper, cardamom, and vanilla
  • Visit the World Coffee Museum (opened 2023)
  • Taste estate-fresh coffee paired with local chocolates at the cafe
  • They also serve traditional Coorg-style lunch (veg & non-veg)
Flat sections & cafe to sit at
1:30 PM Lunch

Coorg Cuisine

Town Center, Madikeri

Hearty and authentic Coorgi food right in the town center. A must-visit for anyone wanting the real taste of Kodagu.

What to do here

  • Must try: Akki Rotti — crispy rice flatbread, a Coorg staple
  • Also try the Bamboo Shoot Curry if you're adventurous
  • Simple and unpretentious — focus is on the food, not the decor
  • Portions are generous so order wisely
3:30 PM Sightseeing

Shri Omkareshwara Swamy Temple

Madikeri

Built in 1820 by King Lingarajendra II. A unique blend of Islamic domes, Gothic arches, and Hindu tradition — unlike any temple you've seen.

What to do here

  • Notice the central dome and four minarets — rare for a Hindu temple
  • Walk around the water tank — very peaceful, great for photos
  • Learn the legend of why the king built it as penance
  • Quick 20-30 minute visit — peaceful and unhurried
Flat temple complex
5:00 PM History

Madikeri Fort

Madikeri Town

Originally built in the 17th century, rebuilt in granite by Tipu Sultan. Home to a museum, a Gothic church, and the iconic stone elephants.

What to do here

  • Photo op with the two stone elephant statues at the entrance
  • Visit St. Mark's Church inside — now the Madikeri Fort Museum
  • Walk the ramparts for views of the surrounding town and hills
  • Free entry — takes about 30-45 minutes to explore
Driver drops at entrance — flat walk inside
7:30 PM Dinner

East End Hotel

Stuart Hill, Madikeri

A legendary British-era establishment on Stuart Hill. Nostalgic old-school charm, beautiful hill views, and the perfect farewell meal.

What to do here

  • Must try: Keema Dosa — their signature dish, locally famous
  • Mutton biryani and meatballs are also beloved
  • Soak in the vintage decor and hill-facing views
  • Relaxed vibe — a comforting way to end the trip
